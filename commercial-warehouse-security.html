Securing Industrial Warehouses in the Downriver Area

Warehouses and industrial facilities face unique security challenges. With expansive square footage, multiple loading docks, and high-value inventory, standard commercial locks are often insufficient. Here is an in-depth look at high-security egress, crash bars, and access control for large commercial facilities.

Perimeter Defense: High-Security Hardware

The first line of defense is the physical hardware on exterior doors and loading bays. Standard padlocks can be quickly defeated by bolt cutters. We recommend heavy-duty, shrouded-shackle padlocks and heavy-gauge steel hasps for rolling doors. Man-doors should be equipped with Grade 1 deadbolts featuring drill-resistant cylinders and reinforced strike plates.

Controlling Access and Flow

Managing who has access to specific areas of a massive facility is critical. Master key systems allow management to carry a single key while restricting employee access to specific zones. For a more modern approach, electronic access control (keypads, card readers, or biometric scanners) allows administrators to instantly revoke credentials, track entry logs, and set time-restricted access.

Life Safety and Egress Compliance

Security cannot come at the expense of safety. Fire codes strictly dictate how emergency exits must operate. Commercial-grade crash bars (panic hardware) ensure that doors remain locked from the outside but allow immediate, single-motion egress from the inside during an emergency.
